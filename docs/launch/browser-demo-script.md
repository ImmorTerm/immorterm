# Demo script — the self-driven browser (45 seconds)

**Setup before recording:** one ImmorTerm terminal, Claude session already
running, a coffee retailer you have bought from before (so "the usual" means
something and you are already signed in). Browser closed. Clean desk, one
take, no cuts if possible — the whole point is that this is real.

---

## Beats

### 0:00 – 0:05 — the ask

**You type:**

> order the usual coffee beans

**Say:** "This is my terminal. Watch what happens when I ask it to go
shopping."

### 0:05 – 0:15 — the browser wakes up

**On screen:** a real Chrome window opens next to the terminal and loads the
store. At the same moment, live frames of the page start appearing on the
terminal canvas — the terminal is literally showing you the website.

**Say:** "Claude just opened a real browser — my browser, my logins — and
the terminal is mirroring every frame. No extension. No tab-switching. I can
see exactly what it sees."

### 0:15 – 0:30 — Claude drives

**On screen:** the canvas updates as Claude searches for the beans, clicks
the product, picks the usual grind and size, and adds to cart. Each click
lands, the page settles, a new frame appears. Let it breathe — the silence
where the terminal is just *doing it* is the demo.

**Say (over the clicks):** "It finds the beans, the grind, the size... and
carts them. I haven't touched the mouse."

### 0:30 – 0:40 — the human moment

**On screen:** Claude reaches checkout and stops. In the terminal it says
it will not touch payment and asks you to finish in the browser window. You
reach over and click Pay yourself.

**Say:** "And here's the rule: it never touches my card. Payment is mine.
One click — done."

### 0:40 – 0:45 — the punchline

**On screen:** order confirmation frame on the canvas, back to the prompt.

**Say:** "For thirty years we've browsed from terminals. This is the
terminal that browses back."

---

## Fallback ask

If the coffee flow is flaky on the day, swap the ask for:

> get me to the Azure PAT page

Same beats: browser opens, Claude clicks through the portal maze to the
personal access token settings, canvas mirrors it, you land on the page in
seconds. Punchline still lands — the portal maze is the joke everyone knows.

## Do / don't

- **Do** keep the browser window and the terminal both visible in frame —
  the mirror is the magic and people need to see both at once.
- **Do** let clicks play out in real time; no speed-ups. Real speed is
  believable speed.
- **Don't** narrate the tool names or say "MCP" or "CDP". The words are
  "Claude opened a real browser".
- **Don't** stage a fake store or a mock page. If it breaks, keep the take —
  Claude recovering from a miss-click is a better ad than perfection.
